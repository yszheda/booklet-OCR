Fugues 1-4 are “simple” fugues—so called only in that special contrapuntal complica-

*tions have been omitted. These fugues, especially 1—3, are written in a style deriving from*

Thematic variation in these fugues is easily audible, Nos. 3 and 4 having inverted subjects.

Fugues 5—7 are “stretto” fugues, in which the subjects’ entries are made to overlap. All three

Up to this point, only “primary” material (i. e., the main theme and variants of it) has been
